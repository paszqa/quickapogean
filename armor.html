Armor

A bunch of tables with examples, which should show how getting more armor affects the damage you receive. A simple concept explained.

This article is based on Feb 2025 Playtest, last updated: 2025 Mar 18

Get som' armor

It can be easily found on neck items, helmets, chests, legs, and boots. It protects you from physical damage and the items can be of Heavy Armor type or Light Armor type.

Armor formula

ReceivedDamage = (Attacker Damage - Armor/2) / (1 + Armor/100)

You start the game with your armor set to 0. Every 2 Armor points reduce physical damage taken by 1 point flat and by a percentage of the damage received. The formula above shows how the damage is calculated after the armor is applied.

As you might have noticed, diminishing returns do apply in that case. If you take a monster which deals 50 Raw Damage, the first 11 points of armor bring the damage down by 10 - to being hit for 40. To bring it down by another 10 to received just 30 damage, you would require another 14 points (Armor 11->25).
Another example - the mitigation of 100 Damage hit. To receive only 75 Damage, you need 20 Armor total. But to get hit for 50, you need to add 30 more Armor, to bring it to a total of 50 Armor.
